## 📌 SECTION 1 — PROBLEM STATEMENT

**The Problem**
Every day millions of people walk into a store or open an app and buy shampoo based on brand name, packaging design, smell, price or influencer recommendation. Nobody asks the most important question — *"Is this right for MY hair?"*

Companies deliberately hide important ingredient information in tiny print because they know if users read it carefully, many would not buy. This creates a massive information gap between what users need and what they actually buy.

The result? People waste money. Hair gets damaged. Users switch products constantly without ever finding what truly works for them. This is not a small problem — this affects every single person who buys shampoo.

---

## 📊 SECTION 2 — USER SEGMENTS

**Four distinct user groups exist in this market:**

**Segment 1 — Infants & Newborns**
Parents make all buying decisions. They need ultra gentle, safe products but are overwhelmed by too many "baby" options with no clear guidance.

**Segment 2 — Children (Age 3-12)**
Parents decide based on price. Neither cheap nor expensive products are chosen based on the child's actual hair or scalp needs.

**Segment 3 — General Adults (Age 18-35)** ⭐ Primary Target
Largest segment. Switch products constantly based on hype, influencer recommendations, sales and discounts. Never stick to one product. Never see real results. Waste money every month.

**Segment 4 — Hair Loss & Balding Users**
Follow doctor route but still confused between medical and consumer products. Highest desperation level. Highest willingness to find the right solution.

## 🔍 SECTION 4 — USER RESEARCH

**Research Method: Personal Observation + Real User Interview**

**Observation 1 — Income Based Buying Pattern**
Through personal observation of buying behavior across income groups:

| Income Group | Buying Trigger | Problem |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Lower middle class | Cheapest option in budget | Compromises hair health for price |
| Middle class | Good but budget conscious | Falls for discount and sale traps |
| Upper class | Premium products only | Falls for elite marketing, still wrong product |

**Key Insight:** All three income groups buy the wrong product. Just for different reasons. This means Hair Checkup is needed by everyone regardless of income.

**Real User Story — Father (Middle Class Adult)**

- Monsoon: Bought Head & Shoulders from DMart during discount
- Used full bottle, saw some improvement
- Next purchase: "Too expensive now" → Switched to Dove
- Winter: Dove worked for dandruff and dryness
- Summer: Switched to ₹1 Clinic Plus sachet due to laziness and price
- Excuse given: *"Shampoo is shampoo, any would work"*

**Pattern Identified:**
Even when a product WORKS — price and laziness cause switching.
User never builds the habit of using the right product consistently.
This is exactly the problem Hair Checkup solves.

## 💡 SECTION 5 — PRODUCT VISION

**Hair Checkup — How It Works**

A smart decision tree based recommendation system that guides users to their perfect shampoo through a simple visual and question based flow.

START
↓
Step 1 — Visual Hair Type Guide
User sees images of different hair types
"Which one looks like your hair?"
Dry / Oily / Normal / Damaged / Curly / Straight
↓
Step 2 — Symptom Checker
Simple Yes/No questions
"Do you have dandruff?" Yes / No
"Does your scalp feel oily?" Yes / No
"Do you experience hair fall?" Yes / No
"Is your hair dry and rough?" Yes / No
↓
Step 3 — Fact Validation
"You selected oily scalp + dandruff.
Does your hair feel heavy by evening?" Yes / No
Confirms user is on right path
↓
Step 4 — Result
"Based on your hair type and symptoms —
here are YOUR top 3 shampoos"
With explanation WHY each one works for you
↓
Step 5 — Ingredient Explainer
"Zinc Pyrithione fights dandruff"
"Salicylic Acid controls oil"
Simple language. No jargon.
↓
END — User found their perfect shampoo

## 🧠 SECTION 9 — MY LEARNINGS

**What I Discovered Building Hair Checkup:**

**Learning 1 — Companies Hide Information Deliberately**
The most shocking insight was realizing that companies hide negative ingredient information in tiny unreadable print. This is not accidental. They know that informed users would choose differently. This confirmed that an information gap product like Hair Checkup has real market need.

**Learning 2 — Human Psychology Cannot Be Simplified**
The hardest challenge was understanding that every person has a completely different buying trigger. Price, smell, brand, looks, ingredients — there is no single pattern. This taught me that personalization is not a feature. It is the entire product.

**Learning 3 — Even Right Decisions Get Abandoned**
My real user research showed that even when users find a product that works — they abandon it for price or convenience. The real problem is not just finding the right shampoo. It is building the habit of using it consistently. This insight would shape Version 2 of Hair Checkup significantly.
